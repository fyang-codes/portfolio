This collaborative project emerged from the need to bridge knowledge gaps between healthcare providers and UX researchers. By bringing together experts from both fields, we aimed to develop more effective, user-centered healthcare interfaces and experiences.

Our collaborative process involved:

  • Initial stakeholder meetings to align objectives across disciplines
  • Mixed-method research combining clinical expertise with UX methodologies
  • Co-design workshops with healthcare providers, patients, and designers
  • Iterative prototyping with feedback from all stakeholder groups

The collaboration yielded significant outcomes:

  • Published research paper in the Journal of Healthcare Experience Design
  • Development of a toolkit for healthcare UX assessment
  • Implementation of findings in two major hospital interface redesigns
  • Establishment of an ongoing cross-disciplinary working group
