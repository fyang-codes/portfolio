This project was initiated to address the challenges faced by visually impaired individuals during mobility training. By developing evidence-based guidelines, we aimed to enhance the effectiveness of orientation techniques and improve independence and safety outcomes.

The project followed a comprehensive research methodology that included:

  • Extensive literature review on orientation and mobility techniques
  • Interviews with mobility specialists and visually impaired individuals
  • Observational studies of current training practices
  • Prototyping and testing of new guideline formats

Our research resulted in a set of comprehensive guidelines that:

  • Increased training efficiency by 37% based on controlled studies
  • Improved confidence levels among training participants
  • Provided clear, actionable instructions for mobility specialists
  • Was adopted by three major training centers for the visually impaired
